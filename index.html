01: Problem
The feed is a trap.
The intros are noise.
01
Compulsive feeds
LinkedIn was built to keep you scrolling, not to help you connect. Your timeline follows you forever, whether you've grown or not.
02
Low-signal intros
Most tools match on similarity: same industry, same title. You end up meeting people who look like you, not the people you actually need right now.
The gap between how professional relationships actually form and what our tools ask us to do has never been wider.
02: Solution
Built for where you are
right now.
Feed
Posts age and fade
Content lives for 21 days then softens. Your history is always yours, invisible to others and revivable anytime.
Intros
AI superconnector
At least one carefully considered introduction every week. Behavioral signal, not just profile data.
Depth zone
The app stops you
Scroll too deep into old content and Lethe notices. A message appears. Gets more direct every time.
03: Product
A feed with a philosophy.
Lethe is a high-fidelity environment for founders, thinkers, and creators. Designed around how connections actually form. Slow enough to be intentional. Smart enough to spark meaningful meets.
The matching is better because the signal is better: behavioral and hyper-personalized. Your feed tells Lethe what you're thinking about this week, not what your job title has been for three years.
Match settings: define the character, interests, and location of your ideal connections before you've posted anything. Cold start solved.
Human vetting: AI suggestions reviewed by humans. Quality before scale.
Communities: spaces built around what you're working on now, not who you used to be. No dead weight.
04: Market
The right moment
for this product.
Why now
AI is accelerating how fast we produce content and how little of it means anything. LinkedIn is drowning in AI-generated posts. People are tuning out. The tools for professional connection haven't kept up. Lethe is built for what comes after the feed.
Precedent
Lunchclub proved the appetite for intentional introductions before it shut down. Lethe takes the thesis further, pairing ephemeral content with AI-powered matching for an audience fatigued by always-on feeds and low-signal networking. That demand never went away. No one has built the right successor.
Addressable market
~50M founders, operators, and independent professionals globally
~10M who would pay for better introductions
$1.2B serviceable market at $10/mo
Primary audience
Operators, builders, independent thinkers, mentors, and creators for whom the quality of their network is a professional asset, not a nice to have.
05: Business model
Free to start.
Pay for depth.
Free tier
Core experience
Ephemeral feed, communities, and base AI matching. The core experience is never paywalled. Lethe earns trust before it asks for money.
Paid tier — $10/mo
Hyperpersonalized matching
Fine-tune introductions by intent, context, and personality signals. The upgrade sells itself the moment someone gets a mediocre intro and thinks "I could have been more specific."
Monetization model keeps the product philosophy intact. We sell depth to the people who want it.
